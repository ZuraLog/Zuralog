# Data Tab

The Data tab is where users go to see all of their health data in one place.

Its job is to answer: **"What does my data actually look like?"**

While the Today tab shows a quick summary of the current day, the Data tab goes deeper. It lets users browse their health metrics over time — daily, weekly, monthly — with charts and visualizations that make the numbers meaningful. Users can see how their sleep, activity, heart health, nutrition, weight, and other metrics have changed across any time range.

The Data tab pulls from every source the user has connected. If a user tracks steps with Fitbit, logs meals manually, and syncs sleep from Oura, it all shows up here under one roof. The data is already deduplicated and merged, so users never see the same activity counted twice from two different apps.

This tab is the "show me everything" screen. It does not interpret the data or offer advice — that is what the Coach and Trends tabs are for. The Data tab simply presents the facts: your numbers, your history, your trends, organized clearly so you can explore them yourself.

It also serves as the health score hub, giving users a single composite number that reflects their overall health picture based on the metrics they track.
