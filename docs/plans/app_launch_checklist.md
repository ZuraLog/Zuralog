## 1. App Store Stuff

The App Store presence is your first impression on potential users. For Zuralog, the challenge is communicating complex AI capabilities in a way that feels accessible and trustworthy. Health apps face higher scrutiny from reviewers, so precision in metadata and compliance documentation is essential.

### 1.1 App Title with Keywords

**Status:** ‚òê Not Started  
**Priority:** Critical

**Description:**  
The app title is the most prominent element in search results and should balance brand identity with discoverable keywords. Apple and Google both index app titles, subtitles, and descriptions for search. For Zuralog, the title should clearly communicate the core value proposition while including relevant keywords that potential users would search for.

**Zuralog-Specific Guidance:**  
The title "Zuralog" is distinctive and memorable, but consider whether a subtitle or keyword variant might improve discoverability. Research keywords in the health, fitness, AI, and personal assistant categories. Terms like "AI Health Coach," "Fitness Tracker," "Health Assistant," and "Activity Tracker" are competitive but relevant. The title field allows up to 30 characters on the App Store, so there is room for "Zuralog" plus a brief descriptor if needed.

**Implementation Notes:**  
- Run keyword research using App Store optimization tools
- Check that the title is not trademarked by competitors
- Ensure the title reads naturally and is easy to pronounce
- Test different title variations with A/B testing if possible

### 1.2 Subtitle

**Status:** ‚òê Not Started  
**Priority:** Critical

**Description:**  
The subtitle appears directly below the app title in search results and is another key indexing opportunity. Apple allows 30 characters; Google Play allows 50 characters. This is prime real estate for communicating your value proposition and including additional keywords that did not fit in the title.

**Zuralog-Specific Guidance:**  
For Zuralog, an effective subtitle might communicate the AI-powered nature of the app while hinting at the integration capabilities. Examples to consider:

- "AI Health Assistant" ‚Äî Direct and clear
- "Your Fitness Command Center" ‚Äî Value-focused
- "Connect Apps. Get Insights." ‚Äî Feature-focused
- "Smart Health Tracker" ‚Äî Simple and accessible

The subtitle should complement the title rather than repeat it. If the title emphasizes the brand, the subtitle should emphasize the benefit.

**Implementation Notes:**  
- Include 2-3 high-value keywords naturally
- Focus on user benefit over features
- A/B test different subtitles if the platform supports it
- Ensure the subtitle makes sense stand-alone if the title is truncated

### 1.3 App Description

**Status:** ‚òê Not Started  
**Priority:** Critical

**Description:**  
The app description is your opportunity to tell your complete story. The first two lines are critical‚Äîthey appear in search results before the "more" button is clicked. **Zuralog-Specific Guidance:**

2. **Solution Overview** (2-3 sentences): Explain what Zuralog does
3. **Key Features** (bulleted list): Highlight the most compelling capabilities
4. **Social Proof** (if available): Mention user testimonials, press mentions, or achievements
5. **Trust Indicators** (closing): Reference privacy, security, and support

**Feature Highlights to Emphasize for Zuralog:**

- Connects with Apple Health, Google Health Connect, Strava, Fitbit, and Oura
- AI-powered conversational interface for logging and insights
- Cross-app reasoning that answers complex health questions
- Voice input for hands-free logging
- "Tough Love Coach" persona for accountability

**Implementation Notes:**  
- Include relevant keywords naturally throughout
- Update description regularly with new features
- Localize description for key markets (see localization checklist)
- Review against App Store Review Guidelines for health apps

### 1.4 Keywords Researched and Added

**Status:** ‚òê Not Started  
**Priority:** Critical

**Description:**  
Keywords determine your discoverability in app store search. Apple uses a dedicated 100-character keyword field (comma or pipeline separated, no spaces). Google Play uses the title, short description, and long description collectively for indexing. Thorough keyword research is essential for organic discovery.

**Zuralog-Specific Guidance:**  
For a health AI assistant, keyword research should cover multiple intent categories:

**Category 1: Health & Fitness**
- health tracker, fitness tracker, workout tracker, activity tracker, step counter, sleep tracker, weight tracker, nutrition tracker, calorie tracker

**Category 2: AI & Smart Features**
- AI assistant, AI coach, smart assistant, voice assistant, AI health, chatbot

1. Use tools like Sensor Tower, AppTweak, or App Annie
2. Analyze competitor app keywords

5. Group keywords by relevance and search intent

**Implementation Notes:**  
- Keywords should be single words or short phrases
- Do not repeat words already in title or subtitle
- Regionalize keywords for different markets
- Review and update keywords quarterly

### 1.7 Privacy Policy URL

**Status:** ‚òê Not Started  
**Priority:** Critical

**Description:**

**Zuralog-Specific Guidance:**

- Any data sharing with third parties (integrations, analytics)

**Data Storage and Security Section:**
- On-device storage practices
- Cloud storage and encryption
- Token security for OAuth connections

**User Rights Section:**
- How to access personal data
- How to delete data and account
- How to revoke integration permissions
- GDPR and CCPA compliance details

**Integration-Specific Disclosures:**
- Apple HealthKit data handling
- Google Health Connect data handling
- Strava, Fitbit, Oura OAuth scopes and data usage

**Implementation Notes:**  
- Host on your primary domain (e.g., zuralog.app/privacy)
- Use HTTPS with valid SSL certificate
- Link prominently in-app (Settings ‚Üí Privacy)
- Update policy when adding new features or integrations
- Have legal counsel review for compliance
- Consider using a privacy policy generator specialized for health apps

### 1.10 Age Rating Completed

**Status:** ‚òê Not Started  
**Priority:** Critical

**Description:**  
Age rating is required for both stores and affects content visibility, parental controls, and regulatory compliance. Apple uses a questionnaire; Google Play allows self-declaration. The rating must accurately reflect content to avoid removal or rating changes.

**Zuralog-Specific Guidance:**  
For Zuralog, as a health and fitness application:

**Google Play:**
- Select "Everyone" as the target age group (most appropriate for general fitness)
- Complete the content rating questionnaire honestly
- Health apps may receive "Low maturity" or "All ages" labels

**Factors Affecting Rating:**
- Does the app contain mature themes? ‚Üí No
- Does it reference alcohol, tobacco, or drugs? ‚Üí No
- Is there user-generated content? ‚Üí Possibly (chat logs stored locally)
- Does it include in-app purchases? ‚Üí Yes (subscriptions)
- Is there scary or disturbing content? ‚Üí No

**Implementation Notes:**  
- Be honest in ratings to avoid future issues
- Prepare for potential family sharing considerations
- If adding social features later, revisit ratings

### 5.4 GDPR Compliance

**Status:** ‚òê Not Started  
**Priority:** Critical

**Description:**  
The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) applies to any app with users in the European Union, regardless of where your company is located. It imposes strict requirements on data handling and user rights.

**GDPR Requirements:**

1. **Lawful Basis:**
   - Consent (for most processing)
   - Contract (for service delivery)
   - Legitimate interest (for security, analytics)

2. **User Rights:**
   - Right to access
   - Right to rectification
   - Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")
   - Right to data portability
   - Right to object
   - Rights related to automated decision-making

3. **Privacy by Design:**
   - Data minimization
   - Purpose limitation
   - Storage limitation

4. **Data Protection Impact Assessment:**
   - Required for high-risk processing
   - Document for health data

5. **Breach Notification:**
   - 72-hour notification to authorities
   - User notification if high risk

6. **International Transfers:**
   - Adequacy decisions
   - Standard contractual clauses
   - Binding corporate rules

**Implementation Steps:**
- [ ] Audit data processing activities
- [ ] Update privacy policy for GDPR
- [ ] Implement consent management
- [ ] Create data subject request process
- [ ] Update data retention policies
- [ ] Ensure data portability capability
- [ ] Train team on GDPR requirements
- [ ] Appoint DPO if required (depending on company size)

**Zuralog-Specific Considerations:**
- Health data is "special category" under GDPR (Article 9)
- Requires explicit consent for health data processing
- Additional safeguards required
- Consider UK GDPR as well (post-Brexit)

**Implementation Notes:**  
- Consult with GDPR-specialized attorney
- Document all processing activities
- Implement cookie consent banner
- Prepare for data subject requests
