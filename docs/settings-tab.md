# Settings

Settings is where users control how Zuralog works for them personally.

Its job is to answer: **"How do I make this app mine?"**

Settings is not a tab in the bottom navigation — it is accessed from the profile area. But it touches every part of the app, so it deserves its own documentation.

**Account and profile.** Users manage their identity here — name, photo, birthday, height, gender, email, and password. They can also store emergency health information (blood type, allergies, medications) that could be useful in a medical situation. Account deletion lives here too, with a deliberate two-step confirmation to prevent accidents.

**Integrations.** This is where users connect and manage their fitness apps and wearables — Strava, Fitbit, Oura, Apple Health, Google Health Connect, and others. Each connected service shows its sync status and can be disconnected at any time. New integrations that are not yet available are shown as "coming soon" so users know what is on the way.

**Notifications.** Users have fine-grained control over every notification the app sends — morning health briefings, smart reminders, activity alerts, wellness check-ins, and quiet hours. The goal is to be helpful without being annoying, and users decide where that line is.

**Coach preferences.** Users configure the AI coach's personality (tough, balanced, or gentle), how proactive it is, and whether it gives short or detailed answers. This lets the coaching experience feel personal rather than one-size-fits-all. Users can also manage the coach's long-term memory from Settings → Coach → Memory: toggle it on or off, view every fact the coach has stored, delete individual memories, or clear everything at once.

**Appearance.** Theme (dark, light, or system), haptic feedback, and tooltip preferences.

**Privacy and data.** Users can see and delete anything the AI has stored about them, export all their data, opt in or out of anonymous analytics, and access the privacy policy and terms of service. The principle is transparency — nothing is hidden, and the user is always in control of their information.

**Subscription.** Users can see their current plan, upgrade, restore purchases, and view billing history.

The Settings area is designed so that a user can set it up once and never think about it again — but when they do come back, everything is easy to find and nothing requires technical knowledge to change.
