Question 1 of several

When someone opens the Today tab, what is the single most important thing you want them to feel or be able to do in the first 2 seconds? This shapes everything — the layout, what sits at the top, and what the floating button does.

A

"See how I'm doing at a glance"

The top of the screen is a clear, at-a-glance summary — score, mood, steps, whatever they track. Like a personal dashboard that opens every morning.

B

"Quickly log something and get out"

The screen is optimized for speed. The floating log button is king. Users tap it, log in 3 seconds, and close the app. The rest of the screen is secondary.

C

"Get a personalized briefing for the day"

The tab reads like a morning paper — AI insights, what's notable today, streaks, goals. It rewards opening. Think of it like a smart coach checking in.

D

"See my progress and feel motivated"

Streaks, completions, goals for today — how much of "today" have I done? It gives a sense of accomplishment and nudges you to finish what's left.

E

"All of the above — it's the home screen"

The Today tab is the entire app's front door. It needs to do everything well — log, summarize, motivate, and inform — depending on what each user needs that day.
