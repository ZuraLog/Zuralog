Meal — Field Breakdown

  • Meal type — Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner, Snack, Pre-workout, Post-workout. Required. Single select. Auto-suggests based on time of day — opening at 8am defaults to Breakfast.
  • What did you eat? — Free text description. Required. Not a structured food database — just plain language like "chicken salad with avocado." The AI reads this for pattern detection over time.
  • Rough calories — Optional quick estimate using preset buttons (~300, ~600, ~900, ~1200) or a custom number. Not precise tracking — just a rough ballpark that helps the AI and feeds into the Calories snapshot card on Today. optional
  • How did it make you feel? — Chips: Energised, Satisfied, Heavy, Bloated, Nauseous, Hungry still. This is the most valuable field for AI insights — e.g. "You feel bloated after meals tagged with Takeaway." optional
  • Tags — High protein, Healthy, Takeaway, Home cooked, Cheat meal, Alcohol. Feed the AI pattern engine. optional
  • Notes — free text. optional
  • Required fields — Only meal type and description. Everything else is optional and clearly labelled.
Why no food database or macro tracking? MyFitnessPal and Cronometer already do this exceptionally well and people who track macros use them. Adding a barcode scanner and food database to Zuralog would take months and duplicate a solved problem. Our angle is different — we care about how food makes you feel and what patterns emerge over time, not the exact gram of protein in your chicken breast.
