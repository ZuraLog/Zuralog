How the toggle works

  • Quick mode ON — Screen collapses to just meal type + a big calorie input with preset buttons. Two taps and done. Perfect for snacks, fast food, anything where the user just wants a number.
  • Quick mode OFF — Full meal log with description, rough calories, how it made you feel, tags, and notes. Richer context for the AI.
  • App remembers your preference — After the first use, the screen opens in whichever mode you last used. remembered A power user who always does full mode never sees the toggle flipped on. A calorie-only user never has to flip it off again.
  • MyFitnessPal connected? — In full mode, a banner shows that calories will sync automatically. The calories field becomes optional — the sync handles it. In quick mode, the banner appears below the meal type as a soft note.
  • Same data model underneath — Both modes save a meal entry. Quick mode just has fewer fields filled in. The Today snapshot, AI insights, and Health Score treat them identically.
  • Calories field is always optional in full mode — Clearly labelled. Users who describe their meals but don't track calories don't have to enter a number. optional
Why this is the right design: It gives every type of user exactly what they need without asking them to compromise. The calorie-only user gets a frictionless 2-tap experience. The meal journaler gets full context. The MyFitnessPal user gets automatic sync. And none of them have to see a cluttered grid with two "food" tiles. One tile. One screen. One toggle.
